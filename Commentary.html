Did hospitals bring
this on themselves
I don’t shortchange the
stupidity of the republican
establishment, nor do I ignore
the levity afforded by the
parade of clowns that they
have come up with. However I
don’t think that they deserve all
the credit for the current
pandemic of the unvaccinated.
There’s a visibility issue.
No one other than healthcare
providers could see the
pandemic’s pain, suffering, and
end game. No one!
Even worse visitations from
family & close friends were
Completely Stopped. The
ordinary, usual close
interactions with, support for,
and affection towards loved
ones were disallowed. We had
to helplessly stand by while
they struggled against the virus
remotely in enforced
anonymity.
Of course I understand all
about quarantining and
isolation but let us also
understand that there is a cost
to that. During the Plague in
Europe bodies were thrown out
onto the street and the morgue
wagons came through and
collected them. I’ll bet you
would agree that anything
approaching that would take
care of the anti-maskers /
unvaccinated who are
overwhelming our healthcare
structure at the moment.
It is Not a tenable arrangement
to continue to shutdown
society at large in support of
our healthcare structure. No
slight on what had to be done
and was done when this first
happened, but that’s not going
to work long term, going
forward. It is Not any kind of a
solution.
I hope my comments make the
unvaccinated seem less
unreasonable to the rest of us.
It’s not their fault that the
pandemic’s consequences
aren’t playing out in the
community. You tend to deny
that which you cannot see.
There seems to be a clear need
to greatly put what the virus
does more in front of the
community. Not only would it
help us be more focused and
attentive but it would lessen
diveseness.
Let’s talk about how best to do
that: “open ward hospice”,
“throwing extremist virus
deniers out of the Emergency
Room when they show up to be
treated for it”, “greatly relaxing
acute/intensive care visiting
restrictions”, “more in depth
reporting on the consequences
of what Covid infection does”,
and “Anything other than
limping along the way we’ve
been doing”.
