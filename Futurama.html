Futurama

One time I looked up the first time Moore’s Law was presented. It turned out to be December 2nd 1964 (my 15th birthday). The article referenced an interview with him in which he indicated his motivation for formulating it was to “sell more chips”.

Fast forward to today! In the second quarter of the twenty-first century chips cannot be supplied fast enough and by supplied I mean by Taiwan. The horror story being that Mainland China’s threats to that arrangement are completely unthinkable. Maybe the conventional wisdom is a little off-base here.

Sure it would mess up the release cycle of the next “great” iPhone, but is that such an Armageddon?

How much would it affect the economy in real life? Nobody actually needs self-driving cars, crypto currency, or Ai with their insatiably increasing demand for chips. If you look around you doesn’t it seem as if the economy only needs four things: Nail Salons, Delivery Guys, Gasoline, and Grocery Stores? None of them need chips.

The others are insidious make-work schemes for chips. The closer you get to self-driving cars the more computational moxie you are going to need to put in each of them. Crypto currency started out innocently enough, anyone could mine them with a PC and to earn an amount crypto currency. There was a rub however: the mining algorithm got increasingly complex with crypto’s popularity. All of the sudden you needed a data center or you couldn’t play. Then you needed a larger one, and so on and so on. I sort of wonder whether or not Ai voracious appetite for computer farms was modeled on Crypto. They certainly have the same effect.

Keep in mind also that, unlike like batteries, chips don’t wear out. More than enough of them already exist and we could recycle them as a reusable resource. For example look at the number of chips based on Arm designs produced to date. It is north of 300 billion! That’s more than 37 chips for every human being alive on Earth in 2026.
